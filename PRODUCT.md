# 4blue2brown Product Context

## Register

Brand / research microsite. The page communicates a research project and its evidence trail rather than serving as an operational dashboard.

## Audience

Primary readers are ML, graphics, and systems researchers who are new to this repo. They may know LLMs, Manim, or educational animation, but should not need prior context from the working thread to understand the project.

## Product Purpose

4blue2brown explores how to turn papers and code into precise, inspectable, rerenderable Manim explainers. The project uses programmatic animation as the controllable substrate, then adds planning, rendering, frame inspection, repair, and evaluation loops around it.

## Positioning

The goal is not to clone a channel identity. The project borrows from the public Manim production model: code-backed visual explanations are easier to inspect, repair, and ground than raw pixel video. The research question is whether an agentic pipeline can produce useful paper/code explainers with measurable quality gates.

## Voice

Clear, careful, and evidence-led. The writing should help a smart newcomer distinguish what is proven, what is a local prototype, and what remains planned. Avoid hype. Avoid burying caveats. Use concrete nouns such as "full upstream run", "style-preview render", "in-house clip", and "manifest".

## Strategic Principles

- Show the pipeline before showing individual artifacts.
- Separate reference landscape from actual coverage evidence.
- State whether a result came from a full external framework or from a baseline-style local renderer.
- Treat render success as a low bar, not the definition of teaching quality.
- Make missing work visible: API credentials, full upstream runs, model comparison, geometry metrics, and learner-bench evaluation.

## Anti-References

- Do not imitate 3Blue1Brown branding, voice, assets, or channel identity.
- Do not present style-preview renders as full framework outputs.
- Do not over-index on raw video count without explaining evidence quality.
- Do not make the page read like a private lab notebook; it should be legible as a public progress brief.
